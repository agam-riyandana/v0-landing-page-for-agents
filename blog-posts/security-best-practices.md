# Security Best Practices for Your BAYARKITA Account

Your account security is our top priority. This guide provides practical steps to protect your BAYARKITA account and ensure safe transactions every time.

## 1. Create a Strong Password

### Password Requirements
A strong password should include:

- **Minimum 12 characters** - Longer passwords are harder to crack
- **Mix of uppercase and lowercase** - Use both A-Z and a-z
- **Numbers and special characters** - Include @, #, $, %, &, etc.
- **No personal information** - Avoid birthdays, names, addresses

### Examples of Strong Passwords
- `B@yar2026!SecurePass#123`
- `MyBiz$2026%Secure@Bank`
- `Agent#Profit2026$Digital`

### Avoid These Mistakes
- ❌ Using your name or business name
- ❌ Sequential numbers like 123456
- ❌ Repeating characters like 111111
- ❌ Common words from the dictionary
- ❌ The same password across multiple sites

## 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-Factor Authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring two verification methods.

### Setting Up 2FA

1. Go to **Account Settings**
2. Select **Security**
3. Click **Enable Two-Factor Authentication**
4. Choose your preferred method:
   - **SMS Code** - Receive codes via text message
   - **Email Code** - Receive codes to your email
   - **Authenticator App** - Use apps like Google Authenticator

### Why 2FA Matters
Even if someone gets your password, they can't access your account without the second verification factor. This prevents unauthorized access.

## 3. Protect Your Personal Information

### Never Share These
- Your password
- Your PIN or security code
- Your account recovery email
- Your phone number associated with your account
- Confirmation codes or OTPs

### BAYARKITA Will Never Ask For
- Your password via email or phone
- Your full credit card number
- Your login credentials
- Sensitive personal data through unsecured channels

## 4. Use Secure Devices and Networks

### Device Security
- **Keep software updated** - Install security patches and updates
- **Use antivirus software** - Protect against malware
- **Lock your device** - Use passwords or biometrics
- **Don't lend devices** - Avoid unauthorized access

### Network Security
- **Avoid public WiFi** - Don't access your account on public networks
- **Use VPN if needed** - Virtual Private Networks add extra protection
- **Verify HTTPS** - Check for the padlock icon in your browser
- **Log out properly** - Always logout when finished

## 5. Monitor Your Account Activity

### Regular Checks
Review your transaction history weekly:

- Check for unauthorized transactions
- Verify all login locations
- Review connected devices
- Monitor login timestamps

### What to Look For
- Unfamiliar transaction amounts
- Transactions from locations you didn't visit
- Login attempts at odd times
- Unknown devices accessing your account

## 6. Verify Before Sharing Information

### Spotting Phishing Attempts
Phishing emails/messages try to trick you into revealing information.

**Real BAYARKITA Communication:**
- Comes from official @bayarkita.com email
- Uses proper company branding
- Never asks for passwords
- Includes a secure login link

**Suspicious Messages:**
- Urgent language ("Act now!" "Verify immediately!")
- Poor grammar and spelling
- Requests to click suspicious links
- Asks for personal or financial information

## 7. Safe Transaction Practices

### Before Every Transaction
1. **Verify recipient details** - Double-check phone numbers and names
2. **Confirm amounts** - Ensure the amount is correct
3. **Check network** - Use secure internet connection
4. **Review summary** - Read the transaction details carefully

### Transaction Tips
- Process transactions during daylight hours when you're alert
- Keep transaction receipts for records
- Document all customer interactions
- Report issues immediately

## Common Security Questions

**Q: Is my account REALLY secure?**
A: Yes. We use bank-level encryption (256-bit SSL), PCI DSS compliance, and regular security audits. Your data is protected with multiple layers of security.

**Q: What if I forget my password?**
A: Use the "Forgot Password" option on login. Verify your identity via email, and create a new password. You'll be logged out for security.

**Q: Can I use the same password on multiple sites?**
A: Not recommended. If one site is compromised, all your accounts become vulnerable. Use unique passwords for each important account.

**Q: How often should I change my password?**
A: Change it immediately if you suspect compromise. Otherwise, every 3 months is a good practice, especially for agents handling transactions.

**Q: What's the difference between password and PIN?**
A: Passwords access your account. PINs are usually shorter codes for specific transactions. Never share either one.
